Charm dominance scenarios (spec + examples)
============================================

Purpose
-------
Drive implementation and review of charm comparison logic:

  * **Pareto dominated** — implemented in `scripts/find_pareto_dominated_charms.py`
  * **Budget dominated** — removed for now; rules TBD from this file + your additions

Encoding reference: `charm-encoded-line-format.txt` (seven comma-separated fields).
Jewel data: `decorations.txt` (format: jewel_name, size, skill, skill_level).


Glossary
--------
  * **Native (on talisman)** — skill + level from skill1/skill2 columns only (no decorations).
  * **Skill pair / skill_key** — (skill1 string, skill2 string) as written; Pareto only compares
    lines with the same pair.
  * **Slot triple** — last three fields; larger values mean larger decoration holes on that charm.
  * **Pareto dominated** — same skill_key; every numeric field ≤ on the loser and at least one
    strict < on the winner (component-wise on lvl1, lvl2, slot1, slot2, slot3).
  * **Budget dominated** (informal, to be nailed down) — loser can be discarded because another
    charm + allowed decorations + your build assumptions replicates or beats what the loser
    gave you, without Pareto’s “same two skill columns” requirement.
  * **Baggage** — a native skill on the dominating charm that the loser’s build did not include;
    may or may not be acceptable (e.g. Punishing Draw vs “pure upside” skills).
  * **Strict improvement** — must be better in some dimension you care about (higher native tier
    on a shared skill, extra slot value after paying for jewels, etc.) — exact definition TBD.


================================================================================
PART A — PARETO DOMINATED (current script behavior)
================================================================================

Rule (summary)
--------------
Same (skill1, skill2). Compare (lvl1, lvl2, slot1, slot2, slot3). Winner ≥ loser on all five;
winner > loser on at least one.

Example A1 — clear Pareto (user discussion)
-------------------------------------------
  Loser:  Critical Eye,2,,0,1,0,0
  Winner: Critical Eye,3,,0,1,1,0

  Why: Same skills (Critical Eye + empty second). Higher CE level; slots 1,1,0 vs 1,0,0 (not
  worse on any slot; strictly better on at least one). Safe to treat loser as obsolete vs winner.

Example A2 — script sanity check
--------------------------------
  Loser: Blight Resistance,1,Affinity Sliding,1,0,0,0
  Winner: Blight Resistance,1,Affinity Sliding,1,3,1,0
  Why: Skills & their levels are equal. The winner has two slots. The loser has no slots.

Example A3 — NOT Pareto (different skill columns)
------------------------------------------------
  Line 1: Critical Eye,4,Weakness Exploit,1,1,1,0
  Line 2: Critical Eye,4,,0,3,1,1

  Verdict: NOT Pareto-comparable (different skill2 / skill_key).

  Why: Pareto requires identical (skill1, skill2) strings. These differ on the second skill
  column, so the script correctly ignores this pair for Pareto.

Example A4
-------------------------------------------
  Loser:  Critical Eye,3,,0,1,0,0
  Winner: Critical Eye,3,,0,1,1,0

  Why: Same skills (Critical Eye + empty second). Slots 1,1,0 vs 1,0,0 (not
  worse on any slot; strictly better on at least one). Safe to treat loser as obsolete vs winner.

Example A5
-------------------------------------------
  Loser:  Critical Eye,2,,0,1,1,0
  Winner: Critical Eye,3,,0,1,1,0

  Why: Same skills (Critical Eye + empty second). Slots even out, but winner has higher CE level.
